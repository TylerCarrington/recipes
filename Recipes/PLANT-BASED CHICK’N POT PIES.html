PLANT-BASED CHICK’N POT PIES

Dinner

Difficulty: Easy Source: Earthbalancenatural.com

Ingredients

  • For the Crust:
  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 sticks Earth Balance® baking sticks
  • a few tablespoons of ice water
  • For the Filling:
  • 1 small yellow onion
  • 1/2 c nutritional yeast
  • 2 stalks celery
  • 2-3 carrots
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 3 potatoes
  • chick'n flavoring
  • Rosemary/Sage/Thyme
  • Tofu

Directions

For the crust:

Measure the flour and put it in the fridge.

Cut the baking sticks into smaller pieces and freeze for at least 20min.

Use a fork (or food processor) to cut the butter into the flour.

Once the butter is pea-size and well incorporated, add the ice water a little at a time until the dough can be balled.

Form the dough into two disks, wrap in plastic, and place in the freezer. (Note: we were using ramekins, so it is better to make one disk larger than the other, as you'll need more dough for the bottoms of the pies).

For the filling:

Marinate the tofu. Add hot water to chick'n flavoring, cut tofu into blocks, immerse in the marinade for several hours. Save the marinade. Once the tofu is marinated, bake it at 350 for about 15 minutes on each side, which gives it a little more texture in the pie.

Chop all filling ingredients. In a dutch oven or saucepan, saute the onion and garlic in oil, add carrots and celery, and cook for several minutes. Add the potatoes and broth (from the tofu marinade), let the potatoes and carrots cook and soften slightly. Add nutritional yeast and herbs, cook until veggies are soft and a gravy has formed (add flour if necessary to create gravy). Stir in cubed tofu.

Roll out the dough and line the ramekins, fill with filling. Roll out second disk of dough, use it to top the pies. Cut in vents, and place in oven. Cook for about 30-35 minutes, until bubbly and slightly browned. Let cool for at least 30 minutes before attempting to eat.
